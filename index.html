Our Reliance on Computers

The world is becoming ever more reliant on computers. Many people would probably find it hard to imagine living in a world without them. In fact, 73% of US adults own a desktop or laptop computer, and 68% own a smartphone. But what would be affected if we lost access to computers, for example, because of an EMP attack?

Civilian Impacts

The entertainment industry is dominated by computer technology. Streaming services, digital books, and of course video games. At least 78% of US households own a video game system. But entertainment is not the most important part. Communication has also become increasingly digital. Aside from the use of phones for calling and texting, around 73% of Americans use social media. Losing computers would not only mean losing a large portion of entertainment, but communication as well.

Government Impacts

The US government has relied on computer technology for a long time now, and many important systems are made possible by it. Medicare, social security, other government benefit programs, and more, would likely be impossible without computer technology.

Economic Impacts

As mentioned earlier, computer technology dominates the entertainment industry. But in a broader view, the digital economy produced $3.7 trillion in 2021, which was approximately 7% of that year's GDP. The loss of computers would cut a large portion of the US' GDP if not restored quickly.
